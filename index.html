How the loop works

From "I need help" to "help is on the way" — in seconds.

Most safety apps stop at a text message. At partner resorts, Izzy closes the loop: the skier's SOS lands in the resort's live dispatch queue, and patrol's answer travels all the way back to the skier's pocket.

  1. 1

    A skier calls for help

    One tap, a voice command, or fall detection after a hard impact. The SOS carries live GPS, a 60‑second breadcrumb trail, and who the skier is — stamped from their verified profile, so it can't be spoofed.

  2. 2

    Patrol's phones buzz — in 2–3 seconds

    The SOS becomes a live incident in the resort's dispatch console, and every on‑shift patroller's phone gets a time‑critical push. No answer? Izzy re‑alerts and widens the circle until a human responds.

  3. 3

    A patroller takes it and heads out

    One tap acknowledges and claims the incident. Dispatch watches en route → on scene live, with the skier's location on the map and the nearest patroller in view.

  4. 4

    The skier hears back — even with the phone in a pocket

    "🚨 Ski patrol is responding — help is on the way." The push wakes a locked phone. If it's a kid, every parent is alerted too, and the kid's screen flips to "Mom's on the way."

  5. 5

    Honest by design

    Izzy says "patrol is responding" only when a patroller actually responds — never on a hopeful guess. If patrol can't be confirmed, the skier is told plainly to call 911. Never a fake "you're covered."

The full dispatch loop runs at partner resorts in the Izzy Resort pilot program. Everywhere else, Izzy still sends your emergency contact your live GPS and gives you verified patrol phone numbers — at all 82 supported resorts.

More than SOS

The whole mountain, talking both ways.

The safety loop is the heart — but the same live wire carries the everyday mountain too. What the resort knows, skiers hear. What skiers need, the resort sees.

  • ✓Live lift & trail status — when ops opens, holds, or closes a lift in the console, skiers' apps update in near real time.
  • ✓Resort announcements — "Avalanche control 9–10am," "Lift 7 on wind hold" — pushed straight to skiers on the mountain.
  • ✓Verified patrol lines — tap‑to‑call the resort's real dispatch number, not a web search.
  • ✓One companion who knows it all — Izzy folds resort data into every answer: conditions, grooming, lifts, dining, events.
