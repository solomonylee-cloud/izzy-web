The ops console

One screen that runs the mountain.

A live dispatch console that works in any browser — on the shack iPad, the patrol director's laptop, or the wall. Skier SOS alerts and staff-reported incidents land in the same queue, and everything updates in under a second.

  • ✓Live incident queue & dispatch — assign patrollers, add timeline notes, track acknowledged → en route → on scene → closed, with outcomes and injury severity.
  • ✓Incident wall map — every open incident on a live mountain map, built for the shack iPad.
  • ✓Coverage heatmap — see which sections of the mountain nobody has checked lately: green under 2 hours, amber 2–4, red beyond.
  • ✓Lift & trail status board — open, hold, or close in one click; the change flows straight into skiers' apps.
  • ✓Shift & staffing dispatch — who's clocked in where, per lift and per position, with force clock-out and a 14-hour fatigue safeguard.
  • ✓Staff comms that confirm — broadcast to everyone, one team, or one lift crew; urgent messages require acknowledgment, and you see who has read what, live.
  • ✓Skier announcements — publish "Lift 7 on wind hold" once and it pushes to skiers on your mountain.

Role-aware by design. Patrol, mountain ops, and guest services each see exactly their piece — and every state change writes an append-only audit row with the real actor behind it.

The patrol app

Dispatch in every patroller's pocket.

iOS and Android, built for gloves and goggles. One staff app for patrollers, lifties, and leads — clock in, and the mountain knows you're on.

  • ✓Time-critical incident push — an SOS reaches every on-shift patroller's phone in 2–3 seconds.
  • ✓One-tap "I'm taking this" — acknowledge to claim, then update en route → on scene → closed from the hill.
  • ✓Navigate to the skier — the incident shows their live GPS on the map, with one-tap hand-off to turn-by-turn navigation.
  • ✓Digital sweep logs — log open sweeps, spot checks, and close sweeps in seconds; the coverage map updates itself.
  • ✓Photos & notes on the incident — the report writes itself while you work.
  • ✓Two-way comms — receive scoped broadcasts, acknowledge the urgent ones, reply to the sender.

Never fake a location. On-shift patroller positions refresh every few minutes so dispatch can route the nearest responder — and a bad GPS fix is dropped, never guessed, because a wrong dot could send help to the wrong side of the mountain.

Life-safety engineering

Built like lives depend on it.

Izzy Resort treats every SOS as a life-safety event, and the engineering follows: the system is designed to fail toward alerting a human, never toward silence.

  • ✓Never-drop SOS — an incident is never lost to a push hiccup; alerting keeps retrying around any failure.
  • ✓Escalates until a human answers — re-alerts roughly every minute, widening from on-shift patrollers to all patrol-eligible staff; an unacknowledged incident stays loud on the console, never silently expires.
  • ✓Confirm-first honesty — the skier hears "patrol is responding" only when a patroller actually responds, never from a delivery count.
  • ✓One press, one incident — retries can't duplicate an SOS, and floods can't poison the queue.
  • ✓Accountable to the second — an append-only audit trail records every state change with the real person behind it; staff access is per-resort, least-privilege, with two-factor on sensitive changes.

Supplemental by design: Izzy Resort strengthens your existing patrol and emergency procedures — it never replaces radios, dispatch protocols, or 911.
